The Single-Family Advantage

This analysis carries one important nuance worth understanding. The active and recently leased rental inventory within a mile of the property is composed almost entirely of newer attached townhomes — Silverleaf Oaks, Hamilton Gardens, and Hamlin Reserve. Your home, by contrast, is a detached single-family residence of nearly identical size, age, and bedroom count.

A detached home typically rents at a premium to an otherwise-comparable townhome — for the privacy of no shared walls, a private yard, and a private garage. That premium generally runs 5–15%. The clearest evidence sits inside the data itself:

  • 16282 Merry Alley — a 1,770 sq ft townhome, essentially the same footprint as the subject — listed at $2,850 but leased at $2,650 (93% of list). This marks where the townhome market clears at this size, and establishes the subject's effective floor.
  • 6338 Hamlin Reserve Blvd — listed at $2,795 and leased over asking at $2,900 (103.8%), proving the immediate area will pay up for the right product.

One honest offset: the townhome comps carry a half-bath the subject does not (2.5 baths vs. 2.0). The detached status, private lot, and garage more than compensate, but it tempers the very top of the range — hence a recommended list at $2,850 rather than reaching for $2,900 out of the gate.
